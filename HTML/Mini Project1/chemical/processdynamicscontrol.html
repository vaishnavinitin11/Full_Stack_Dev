Process Dynamics and Control

Process Dynamics and Control is a course that deals with the study of the behavior of dynamic systems, and the design of control strategies to regulate their behavior. The course covers the fundamental principles of process dynamics, including the time-domain and frequency-domain analysis of dynamic systems, as well as the mathematical modeling of dynamic systems.

In the context of process control, the course covers the design and analysis of feedback control systems, including the use of classical control methods such as proportional-integral-derivative (PID) control and frequency response analysis. Additionally, the course may cover modern control techniques such as state-space control and model predictive control.

Other topics that may be covered in the course include the effects of process variability, process optimization, and the use of simulation tools for control system design and analysis.

The course is relevant to students in a variety of fields, including chemical engineering, mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, and materials science. It is typically a core or elective course at the undergraduate or graduate level. Students are typically expected to have a strong background in calculus, differential equations, and linear algebra before taking the course.
